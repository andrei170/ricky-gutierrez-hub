Questions

The Ones An Operator At Your Stage Asks

Short, direct answers. The longer ones we cover on the call.

Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the business. LPP is fulfilling and selling. The daily YouTube live is shipping every market open. The LPP Group Chat is moderated. Adding a 90-day VSL build, dedicated bilingual lead-magnet pages, a premium Cohort tier, and a paid acquisition layer on top of an already-loaded team is how those projects either get postponed by a year or get launched at half the quality. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

One day to film the LPP homepage VSL. One day to film the Funded Trader Operator Cohort positioning. Calendar holds for cohort delivery in Lever 02. The rest is build team work plus you reviewing the copy, creative, and pricing decisions before they ship. Most operators at your stage spend under 8 hours a month during the 90-day build, and under 2 hours a month after the system is in production.

Will the Cohort cannibalise LPP 3.0?

No. The Cohort and LPP solve different problems. LPP is self-paced curriculum plus the Group Chat plus the daily live for traders earlier in the journey at $699 lifetime. The Cohort is operating-room access at the Techbuds HQ for traders who already cleared the curriculum, traded six months consistently, and are ready to scale account size and risk discipline at portfolio level. Different deliverable, different audience, different buying motion. Most Cohort applicants will be LPP graduates moving up the ladder, not net-new buyers choosing Cohort over LPP. Where there's overlap, the Cohort is the explicit "post-LPP" tier. It expands the ladder, it doesn't compete inside it.

How does the bilingual angle actually work in practice?

LPP 3.0 already ships with Spanish CCs across the curriculum. The bilingual angle is real, just unmonetised at the TOFU layer. The build adds a Spanish opt-in landing (/lista), a Spanish nurture flow, Spanish ad variants in Lever 03, and Spanish in-room support for the Cohort. We do not invent a Spanish-language community that doesn't exist. We connect the Spanish-language audience that already watches your daily live to a Spanish-language opt-in path, then into the existing Spanish-CC curriculum. It's an extension of an asset you already ship, not a new product line.

What about the regulatory tone in day-trading education?

Every line of cohort copy and ad copy gets reviewed against the FTC and SEC standards already established by the Warrior Trading settlement and the broader category enforcement context. No specific income claims, no P&L promises, no "make $X with me" framing. The Cohort is positioned as risk discipline plus operator development, not "make money fast." This is how you protect the brand and charge premium pricing in the same motion.
