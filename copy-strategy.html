Foundation

Copy Strategy - Ricky Gutierrez / Learn Plan Profit

Built: 2026-05-16 Frame: Cold first-touch. Operator-grounded, beginner-respecting, regulatory-aware. Investment-niche register throughout. No income claims. No P&L promises. No "make $X with me" framing anywhere.

Voice rules (Ricky's brand)

Tone: beginner-first, calm, instructional. The voice that built a 1.2M YouTube audience in the day-trading-education category did it by being patient, bilingual, and structurally simple. The copy across this package mirrors that register.

Three-word brand anchor: Learn. Plan. Profit. This is the existing brand frame. Used verbatim where appropriate.

Vocabulary that fits the brand: - discipline, plan, plan adherence, structure, routine, journal, watchlist - nasdaq ETFs, blue-chip stocks (the explicit niche guardrail) - beginner, ready, foundation, next step, scale-ready - daily live, the open, the close, the recap - Techbuds Trading HQ, the room, the desk - Spanish-language, bilingual, both languages

Vocabulary that breaks the brand (avoid across all copy): - "easy money," "guaranteed returns," "passive income from trading," "secret strategy," "make $X," "Lambo," "millionaire," "10x your account," "transform your trading" - options, crypto, penny stocks (these are NOT in Ricky's niche) - "unlock," "next level," "game-changer," "transform your" - any P&L claim, any return-rate claim, any "I made $X" framing

Em dashes: zero across the package. Em-dash substitutes are colon, comma, period, parentheses.

Positioning frame (across the package)

Ricky's existing positioning: The bilingual, beginner-first day-trading educator who broadcasts daily live trading at 9:30 AM EST from a physical Arizona HQ. The "Learn Plan Profit" three-step frame. Niche guardrails: nasdaq ETFs and blue-chip stocks only, no options, no crypto.

The wedge AG1 brings: The four-piece funnel infrastructure (VSL, scorecard, cohort, daily-live capture) that sits on top of Ricky's existing brand without changing the brand. AG1 builds infrastructure. Ricky stays the analyst-voice and the trader on the desk.

Three explicit service-mappings to use in copy:

  1. "We build the 5-7 minute VSL that pre-sells LPP for the right buyer."
  2. "We install the readiness scorecard that filters before checkout."
  3. "We write the daily-live email sequence that retains the 9:30 AM EST audience."

Plus the fourth, which is the cohort:

  1. "We build the application and landing for the LPP Operator Cohort - the mid-tier above the $699 course."

These four sentences are the load-bearing claims in the package. Each is a specific install. None are "create a course." LPP exists.

The scorecard-as-mixed-review-fix angle

The recurring third-party criticism of LPP (knowledgeeager.com, tradingreview.net) is fit mismatch, not content. The scorecard install is the structural fix - it filters before checkout, lifts review quality downstream, and routes the early-stage buyer back to the free YouTube content instead of into the $699 course where they will refund-request against a "no refunds" policy and turn into a bad review.

This angle is used explicitly in the outreach DM, the outreach email, and the bottleneck analysis. It is not used in the public landing copy (the landing speaks to the prospective buyer, not to Ricky). The angle is operator-level diagnostic, not buyer-facing marketing.

Pricing language rules

  • No dollar amounts on landing-page.html or hub-page.html for the cohort. Use "mid-tier mentorship pricing," "the application surfaces the cohort cycle's exact pricing," "hint range, not an invoice."
  • The $699 LPP 3.0 price stays as Ricky publishes it on his own surfaces. AG1 does not duplicate the price on the AG1-built hub or landing.
  • Year-1 floor math (the $220K-$620K stacked number) sits in the outreach email and the bottleneck analysis. Not on the public landing.
  • "Mid-four-figure to low-five-figure" is the range hint for the cohort - used only when a range hint is structurally necessary.

Niche-bans (trading-education, applied across the package)

Banned across every public surface in this package: - "easy money" - "guaranteed returns" - "passive income" (in the trading context - the phrase belongs to a different niche) - "secret strategy" - "make money fast" - "double your account," "10x your account," "scale to $X" - "I made $X" / "you'll make $X" / any P&L claim - "no risk," "low risk," "risk-free"

Permitted (with care): - "discipline," "plan adherence," "operator development" - "scale account size with risk discipline" (the cohort frame, qualified) - "the daily live" (the observable transparency hook) - "ready," "too early," "ready for the cohort" (the scorecard routing language)

Investment-niche footer (mandatory on every public surface)

"Not financial advice. Trading involves substantial risk. Results vary by capital, market conditions, and operator execution."

Present in the footer of: - hub-page.html - landing-page.html - the outreach email body - the bottleneck analysis (already present) - the funnel blueprint (already present) - the VSL preview script (closing line, before the CTA) - the email sequence (in the welcome email, in the post-application email, in the cohort onboarding email)

Outreach copy rules (DM + email)

Email (cold first-touch, sender info@andreicopy.net): - Subject line is short, specific, and does not promise income or returns - Opener establishes the observation lane (read the homepage, watched the daily live, walked the Teachable page) - Four enumerated pieces with the service-mapping language verbatim - Year-1 floor math at the end, framed as "incremental on top of the existing $699 line" - Investment-niche footer disclaimer before the sign-off - Standard email sign-off ("- Andrei / AG1 Consulting Ltd")

What does not go in the package

  • Any reference to specific dollar amounts of prior client results that are not publicly attributable
  • Any reference to household-name agency-coach operators by name
  • Any internal documentation filename references
  • Any reference to Ricky's own surfaces as clickable hyperlinks in the DM (only the AG1 hub URL is clickable)
  • Any reference to the $731K NDA result
  • Any em dashes anywhere
