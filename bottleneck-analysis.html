Bottleneck Analysis - Ricky Gutierrez / Learn Plan Profit

Built: 2026-05-16 Frame: Cold first-touch. No prior outreach logged. This is the structural map of the four pieces I would build first if I were stepping into the Learn Plan Profit funnel today. Scope: The gap between a 1.2M YouTube and 294K Instagram audience and the productised funnel they currently exit through - a static homepage, a single $699 Teachable card with no VSL, no readiness filter, no mid-tier behind it, and no email capture for the daily live trading session.

The one-sentence diagnosis

The Learn Plan Profit ladder is one step deep - free YouTube content at the top, the $699 LPP 3.0 course in the middle, and nothing above it - and the buy decision is made on a static text-and-image marketing page with no VSL and no qualification step. The fix is four installs: VSL, readiness scorecard, mid-tier mentorship cohort, and a daily-live email-capture surface.

The four pieces I would build first

One. The VSL on learnplanprofit.net and the LPP 3.0 Teachable storefront is the missing pre-sell.

The current funnel has 1.2M YouTube subscribers and 294K Instagram followers landing on learnplanprofit.net - a static text-and-image marketing page with press logos (Forbes, Business Insider), the "$1 a day with coupon 1ADAY" framing, and direct CTAs to Teachable. From there they hit a Teachable storefront with a curriculum bullet list and a "Sign Up" button. There is no embedded video on either surface.

The bridge from the YouTube voice to the buy decision is missing. The voice that built the audience - the daily live trading from the Arizona HQ, the beginner-first explanations in English plus Spanish CCs, the "Learn Plan Profit" three-step frame - is not present on the surfaces where the buy actually happens. The audience that converts on Ricky's voice is being asked to convert on Ricky's bullet points.

The fix is a 5-7 minute homepage VSL that pre-sells LPP for the right buyer. Structure: open with the daily-live opener (the format the audience already trusts), state the Learn Plan Profit thesis in Ricky's voice, walk three LPP graduate case studies (named or anonymised per regulatory comfort, no income claims, focus on the discipline outcome), state the scorecard step, then route to enrolment. A Spanish-language variant of the same VSL slots into the bilingual TOFU lane.

This is a specific infrastructure install. AG1 builds the 5-7 minute VSL that pre-sells LPP for the right buyer. We do not build the course. The course exists.

Year-1 floor on this lever: - Conservative VSL-attributable lift on the existing checkout flow at 8-15% incremental conversion on the existing buyers who currently bounce off the static page - Estimated $50K-$120K Year-1 incremental on the $699 LPP 3.0 line alone, attributable to the VSL pre-sell - Compounds against every YouTube CTA, every IG link-in-bio click, and every Spanish-language entry point

Two. The readiness scorecard is the missing qualification step - and the cleanest fix for the mixed-review problem.

The third-party review ecosystem on LPP (knowledgeeager.com, tradingreview.net, the 2025-2026 review cycle) recurs on a single criticism: "light on advanced strategy, heavy on basics," "good for beginners, less useful once you're already trading," "no clear next step after finishing." That is not a content problem. That is a fit-mismatch problem. Anyone with $699 and a working card can buy LPP regardless of where they actually are on the trader-readiness curve. The course is rated by people who should never have been allowed into it without a stage check.

The fix is a 90-second "Are You Ready For Day Trading?" scorecard between the YouTube / IG audience and the LPP checkout. Structure: 10-12 questions across capital available, trading experience to date, time available for the daily live, risk tolerance, prior course experience, current trading PnL stage (in bands, not exact numbers). Returns a 0-100 readiness score plus a 3-line bottleneck readout plus a routing recommendation - "you are ready for LPP 3.0" / "you are ready for LPP plus the cohort" / "you are too early - here is the free daily live and the YouTube playlist." Captures email at the moment of scoring. Tags by score band, by capital band, by language preference (English / Spanish).

The scorecard does four things simultaneously: - Filters fit-mismatched buyers out of LPP before they purchase, which lifts review quality downstream - Captures email at scale from the 1.2M YouTube and 294K IG audience for the first time - Tags the warm slice (high-readiness, high-capital) for the cohort tier - Routes the early-stage slice back to the free YouTube content instead of into a $699 course they will refund-request (which the "no refunds" policy converts into bad reviews)

This is a specific infrastructure install. AG1 builds the scorecard, the scoring logic, the routing, and the email-tagging integration. We do not write the curriculum. LPP is the curriculum.

Year-1 floor on this lever: - Email capture at 4-7% of cold YouTube + IG traffic into the scorecard surface: 30-60K incremental Year-1 subscribers, segmented and tagged at the moment of capture - LPP review quality lift from filtering fit-mismatched buyers (compounds against every future ad spend by lifting baseline conversion) - $30K-$80K Year-1 incremental on the $699 line through scorecard-routed LPP conversions, plus the warm-tagged cohort fills that route downstream

Three. The mid-tier mentorship cohort is the unfilled slot between $699 and the implied higher-touch lane.

The Learn Plan Profit ladder ends at $699. The 1,000+ LPP Group Chat alumni are the warmest possible list for a tier above the course - and currently have no upgrade path. The Techbuds Trading HQ is a physical facility in Arizona broadcasting the daily 9:30 AM EST live. That facility is the moat. No other day-trading operator at this audience tier has a physical in-person HQ already running.

Every peer in the category fills this slot. Ross Cameron's Warrior Inner Circle at $9,997 application-only, capped at ~100 traders. Tim Sykes Millionaire Challenge at $3-5K/yr application-gated. Mark Minervini Master Trader at $4-5K plus $6K/yr Private Access. Cameron Fous FOUS4 mentorship priced privately. The slot above $699 in the day-trading-education category is structurally established and sitting unfilled in the LPP ladder.

The fix is a productised mid-tier mentorship cohort. Working name: The LPP Operator Cohort (lock with Ricky on day one of the install). Format: 6-8 verified day traders, mid-four-figure to low-five-figure price per operator, application-gated, capped, with a 2-day in-person component at Techbuds Trading HQ + 90-day implementation runway. Filter: LPP graduate or equivalent training, currently trading live (not paper), ready to scale account size with portfolio-level risk discipline. Bilingual seats welcome (English / Spanish in-room support available, leveraging the existing Spanish CC infrastructure).

The cohort respects the beginner-first brand. It is positioned as "post-LPP, ready-to-scale, risk-discipline at the operator level" - not "advanced-only," not "make $X with me." The Arizona HQ is the moat, the application is the filter, the scorecard from Piece Two is the upstream router.

This is a specific infrastructure install. AG1 builds the cohort landing page, the application form (Typeform uTtFirS4), the scorecard-to-cohort routing, the post-application email sequence, the in-person logistics surface, and the post-cohort alumni email cadence. We do not run the cohort. Ricky runs the cohort.

Year-1 floor on this lever: - 4 cohorts / yr · 6 seats avg · mid-four-figure to low-five-figure price = mid-six-figure floor - Conservative model: 4 · 6 · $5,000 = $120,000 floor; 4 · 6 · $10,000 = $240,000 base; 4 · 6 · $15,000 = $360,000 upside - Floor scenario is built on conservative seat fill (75% of cap) and floor pricing - Compounds against every scorecard-tagged high-readiness segment for the life of the list

Four. The daily 9:30 AM EST live trading session is the missing email-capture and retention surface.

The daily live trading at 9:30 AM EST on YouTube is the deepest transparency hook in the category. It is also the largest unmonetised retention surface in the funnel. Anyone who shows up, watches, and leaves is invisible to nurture. No pre-live email opt-in ("get tomorrow's watchlist before the open"), no post-live recap email, no retargeting list built off the live URL.

Each daily live drives observable engagement - chat activity, live concurrent viewers, replay views - and routes zero of that engagement into an owned email list. Five mornings a week, the largest free trading session in the day-trading-education category fires into the void.

The fix is a two-part install: - Pre-live opt-in. A simple landing ("Get tomorrow's watchlist before the 9:30 AM open") captures email, gates the pre-open watchlist as a daily PDF or email asset, sets the retargeting pixel. - Post-live recap email. Daily-cadence email to the captured list: 3-bullet recap of the live, the trade log (no PnL claims, the discipline log), tomorrow's setups, the next LPP enrolment touchpoint.

This is a specific infrastructure install. AG1 builds the opt-in surface, the daily recap email template, the retargeting pixel setup, and the segmentation tags. We do not write the watchlist. Ricky's analyst voice writes the watchlist.

Year-1 floor on this lever: - Conservative daily-live capture at 2-4% of live attendance plus replay views into the opt-in surface - 10K-25K incremental Year-1 subscribers, tagged as daily-live-engaged (the highest-intent slice of the audience) - $20K-$60K Year-1 incremental from list-driven LPP fills, scorecard re-engagements, and cohort routing

What this is not

  • This is not a course build. LPP 3.0 exists and is the curriculum.
  • This is not an advisory engagement. The mid-tier cohort is operator development and risk discipline, not "trade my signals for $X."
  • This is not a re-platform. learnplanprofit.net stays. Teachable stays. The four installs sit on top of the existing infrastructure without dismantling it.
  • This is not a refund-policy change. The "no refunds" policy stays - the scorecard upstream is the filter that makes the policy fair.

Risk register

  • Regulatory tone. Every cohort and landing surface stays at the level of risk discipline and operator development. No income claims, no P&L promises, no "make $X with me" framing. Footer disclaimer present on every public surface.
  • Beginner-first brand respect. Cohort copy positions the mid-tier as the natural next step for the LPP graduate who is ready to scale, not as "advanced only."
  • No fabricated tech. Every gap listed is observable from learnplanprofit.net, learn-plan-profit.teachable.com, the @rickygutierrezz IG bio, the YouTube channel, and the public third-party review ecosystem.
  • Mixed-review reality. The scorecard install is the structural fix for the recurring fit-mismatch criticism, not a marketing fix.
